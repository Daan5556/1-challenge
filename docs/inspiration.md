# Inspiration

**Author:** Daan Eggen  
**Date:** 16/04/2026  
**Version:** 1.0

---

This document describes what inspired me to dive deeper into embedded systems.
At work I mainly write software, but that software interacts with embedded
devices built by my colleagues. Because of that, I got more and more curious
about the systems on the other side of the interface.

I noticed that I did not only want to consume the data coming from these
devices, I also wanted to understand how the devices themselves work. That
curiosity made embedded systems more interesting to me over time.

## Influence from work

One of the biggest reasons I got inspired is the people around me at work. Some
of my colleagues create embedded devices that I interact with through the
software I write. That means I regularly see the result of their work in a very
practical way.

Because of that collaboration, embedded systems stopped feeling abstract. I
could see how firmware, sensors and communication protocols directly affect the
software layer. That made me want to learn more about the full chain, from the
physical device up to the application.

## Working with physical systems

Another part that inspired me is that embedded systems let me manipulate real
and physical things. In regular software development, the results often stay on
the screen. With embedded systems, a change in the code can lead to something
happening in the real world.

I like that direct connection between software and hardware. Reading a sensor,
toggling a pin or measuring something from the environment makes the work feel
very tangible. It gives immediate feedback and makes experimentation more
exciting.

## Why I want to go deeper

Because I already interact with embedded devices in my daily work, learning more
about embedded systems feels like a natural next step. It helps me understand
the constraints of the devices, communicate better with colleagues and build
better software around the hardware.

At the same time, I am motivated by the fact that embedded development deals
with real objects and real behavior. That combination, professional exposure and
the ability to manipulate physical things, is what pushed me to explore embedded
systems in more depth.
